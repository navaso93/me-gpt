# Education

## Available Education Information ordered chronologically
Marc has completed education programs in several countries and areas, bringing to his background a wide international mix that Marc uses now to adapt to new environments and situations easily.
Marc's education can be ordered as below.
During childhood and teenage years:
- At four years old, Marc enters the 'Lycée Francais de Barcelone', Bon Soleil, located in Gavá, next to Barcelona, his hometown.
- Marc would study at Bon Soleil until he his 18 and goes to university.
- Marc was an average student during most of his time at school, but always showed interest in STEM topics despite the grades in those being average too.
- At sixteen years old, when having to decide which speciality to pursue in order to prepare for university, Marc chooses Maths, Physics, Chemistry and Biology, in line with his interests.
- At sixteen years old, Marc did not know yet what career he wanted to pursue, but had a taste for STEM topics rather than more social subjects.
- The last two years of school, from sixteen to eighteen years old, Marc improved his grades substantially, as if holding himself accountable for his choice of pursuing STEM topics
- At eighteen years old, Marc faces his university access exams in June 2011
- His bachelor choices were, in order:
  1. Mechanical Engineering at UPC (Universitat Politecnica de Catalunya)
  2. Physics at UB (Universitat de Barcelona)
  3. Information Technology at UPC (Universitat Politecnica de Catalunya)
- Marc scores an enough good grade in his university access exams so that he can enter his first option: Mechanical Engineering at UPC

Bachelor in University, from 2011 to 2016:
- Marc begins his university in September 2011.
- During his early university years Marc manages very well to adapt to the change from going to a private school with teachers kind of protecting students, to a public university with more than a hundred students per class where no special attention is given from teachers.
- Marc had to work hard to overcome some difficulties in topics he never really treated before, like 'informatics' or 'material sciences and technology'.
- During the entire bachelor, Marc only had to repeat three subjects: 'Science and technology of Materials', 'Mathematics: Second module' (which mainly involved algebra) and 'Electronic Systems'.
- During his second year, Marc started getting interested by energy and especially renewables. His passion for nature also made it a relatable subject to which he wanted to contribute.
- From February to August 2015, Marc completed an Erasmus semester abroad in Milan, completing several subjects in Politécnico di Milano, mostly about Mechanical Engineering.
- During the erasmus semester, all subjects except one were taught in italian, and Marc had to quickly improve his italian skills to follow the courses.
- Being fluent in French, Spanish and Catalan helped Marc become fluent in Italian before finishing the semester abroad. Now Marc is still completely fluent in this language.
- After the semester abroad, Marc returns to Barcelona to finish the bachelor in Mechanical Engineering with a final degree project consisting on "Design and Manufacturing of a 500W Residential Windmill"
- The project was developed together with a peer and completed in january 2016 with a score of 8.5/10.
- By completing this project, Marc graduated from his bachelor in Mechanical Engineering.



Double Masters degree from 2017 to 2019:
- After a year and a half travelling abroad, Marc got accepted into the 'KIC InnoEnergy Masters Program', a double masters degree lasting two years with one year spent in two different universities.
- Marc got accepted as a tuition fee waiver candidate for the 'Energy for Smart Cities' program.

  - The project (Heat Boxing), was developped with a couple fellow students and allowed Marc to push and widen his knowledge about thermodynamics from a business perspective, as this challenge was proposed by a real company (Indaver, located in Belgium).
  - Marc's team came with the idea of trying to distribute excess heat to nearby households via highly thermaly conductive pipes.
- Marc started his second year

### Education after Masters degree

After the masters degree, Marc moved to Berlin and started his full-time career, which took time away from him to complete further educational programs.
- As Marc's career in solar industry grew, he started getting more interested in Data, tech, and all the potential hidden there.
- In his day to day work, Marc started feeling that if he knew better how to work with large volumes of data, he could exponentially increase his impact in the company and the sector.
- With the intention of learning about data and tech in mind, Marc pursued several online courses, sometimes financed by himself or by his employer, using ressources such as Coursera, Udemy or others.

### Le Wagon's data cience & AI bootcamp

After leaving his full-time job, Marc enrolled in an intensive Data Science & AI bootcamp, provided by Le Wagon.
- In this bootcamp, Marc spent more than three-hundred hours intensively solving challenges related to Machine Learning, Deep learning, Neural Networks, Time series, and other related topics.
- As a final project, Marc proposed tot he class to work on an app that serves as a 'Political Statement Trustworthiness Predictor'.
- Marc and his team trained several models on a dataset of more than 35_000 political statements, categorized with 6 different labels of trustworthiness.
- The models managed to get more than 80% accuracy and the final product was presented on the final day (demo day) succesfully.
- The reason Marc chose this project was because Marc wanted to dive deeper into Natural Language processing methods, which he felt were more complicated than purely numerical ones.

After completing Le Wagon's bootcamp, Marc is commited to deepening his knowledge in Data, Machine Learning and AI, and use it to exponentially impact the PV sector.
